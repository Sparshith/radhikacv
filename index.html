Why I Do What I Do

I’m Radhika Radhakrishnan, 23. I am passionate about research on how we can achieve gender equality through equitable access to technology and directed policies.
I hail from a society where gender inequalities remain deeply entrenched in every sphere. While individual experiences are easy enough to name, their cumulative impact establishes the creeping understanding of what it means to be female in this culture; an understanding that has shaped my reality.
During university, I took to parliamentary debating, and traveled extensively across Asia, engaging with individuals and their diverse worldview. Understanding how the world works helped me contextualise my own position in it and reflect on it critically, leading to the realisation that I am not defined by my experiences of gender discrimination. Being connected and heard within a global community provided me the courage and support to become a change agent at home. To better understand how this change would play out in a real world scenario, I studied public policy after graduation, making policies with people who were already creating ripples in their fields.
Coming from a background of technical education and research, I have come to understand the power of technology in helping people imagine new possibilities and making them happen. But I also realised that women are being left behind in this revolution. Communication technology is a key enabler of rights - a gateway to accessing rights such as freedom from gender discrimination. Hence, my technical research has always focused on harnessing its significance and potential in enhancing the lives of people that can benefit from equitable access the most.
“Given all that women are expected to live with, I can’t help but wonder what it all has done to us. Not just to how women experience the world, but how we experience ourselves. Who would I be if I didn’t live in a world that hated women?” - Jessica Valenti
This is not the world I want to live in. This is why I do it.
